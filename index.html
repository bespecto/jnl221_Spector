Response to Hip Hop Unique Words Piece

In the hip hop vocabulary article, Matt Daniels uses a multitude of dataviz elements to express that hip hop artists use the most unique lyrics out of any other genre. The data itself was first produced in 2014, but was then recently updated in 2019. Daniels starts with a dataviz element similar to that of a plot chart. It has pictures of a bunch of different artists and on the x axis has the number of unique words. This chart is interactive, which allows us to visually see and determine which artist uses a certain amount of unique words. The finding from the chart though is fairly simple, but the way it is presented is easy-on-the-eye and visually appealing. It also creates deeper meaning since it puts names to faces for people unfamiliar with artists in the hip hop genre.

The next chart is a simplified version of the previous interactive visual representation. It separates the amount of unique words into ranges, and puts the rapper’s names where they fall in that range. This allows the previous chart to be better comprehended. Additionally, it is another way to show that certain artists use a ton of unique words compared to others. This chart has the look of a symmetrical bar chart, but the content is different and more creative given that there aren’t individual bars for each artist.

The dataviz elements in this project go deeper than just which artist uses the most unique words. There is a chart at the end of the piece that shows how many unique words are used in hip hop compared to any other genre. The graphic is a simple histogram with a header and labels. There is no subhed, but tons of context in the surrounding pieces of text. The colors make the image easy-on-the-eye and easy to understand.
